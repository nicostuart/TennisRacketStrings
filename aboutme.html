About Me

My name is Nico Stuart, and I am a current senior at WashU studying Business and Computer Science. Outside of the classroom, I find myself engaged with numerous extracurricular activities, including the ski club, engineering teaching assistant work, volunteer running with the visually impaired, intramural tennis and more!

Since I was young, I have thoroughly enjoyed many high-energy activities and sports. The quick pace of tennis naturally drew me in quite quickly. What's more, I appreciated the ability tennis offered me to play conversationally as well as competitively. In other words, I could casually rally and hit the ball back and forth with my parents while talking and catching up with them. At the same time, I could walk on a court while on the highschool tennis team with competitive spirits high, and put my full effort into every shot.

Tennis offered me the best of both worlds.

Interest in Researching and Building this Page

Throughout my life I have had a passion for coding (particularly website development and video game development) as well as the sport of tennis. In considering topics to research, I came accross a report by the US Tennis Association. The report attempted to depict the growing nature of tennis, highlighting that a record-breaking roughly 25.1 million Americans, ages 6 and older who didn't play tennis in 2023 were "very interested" in dong so now [1]. What's immediately clear, is that the sport will continue to see significant growth in the in the near future.

While growth for the sport is certainly beneficial in many ways (for example, the report further stated that massive investment in new technologies will likely supplement the growing player base), new players who pick up the sport will need to purchase tennis rackets and strings to suit their play. I recognized the need for a centralized website that was both informative and one that produced a tangible reccomendation, hopefully eliminating the step of receiving outside consultation for the tennis racket string purchases.

I have worked hard to produce a website that is not only simple and easy to use, but also has some interactivity. I suspect as a prospective player reads this website, her anticipation will steadily build to get out on the court - and to solve that, I want to make this site enjoyable to interact with!
